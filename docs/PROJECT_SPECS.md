# SNAP-AI: Strategic Project Specifications

## Executive Summary

**SNAP-AI** is a state and county-level AI platform designed to accelerate SNAP (Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program) application processing and reduce benefits delivery backlogs. Starting with Cumberland County, North Carolina, the system uses intelligent automation to help eligibility workers process applications faster while maintaining strict compliance with federal SNAP regulations.

---

## Problem Statement

### Current SNAP Processing Challenges

1. **Application Backlogs**: Counties nationwide struggle with application backlogs of 30-90+ days
2. **Federal Timeliness Standards**: SNAP requires 30-day processing (7 days for expedited cases)
3. **Staffing Shortages**: High turnover and vacancy rates in county social services
4. **Complex Eligibility Rules**: Federal and state regulations create complex verification requirements
5. **Document-Heavy Process**: Multiple documents required for income, residency, identity, assets
6. **Manual Data Entry**: Repetitive data entry from paper applications into state systems
7. **Hungry Families Wait**: Delays mean families go without food assistance they need and qualify for

### Business Impact
- **Federal Penalties**: States face sanctions for low timeliness performance
- **Client Hardship**: Vulnerable populations delayed in receiving food assistance
- **Worker Burnout**: Eligibility workers overwhelmed with high caseloads
- **Error Rates**: Rushed processing leads to eligibility errors and improper payments
- **Political Pressure**: County commissioners and state legislators demand faster processing
- **Reputation Risk**: Public criticism of county government effectiveness

---

## Solution Overview

### What SNAP-AI Provides

**AI-Powered Application Processing Acceleration**  
SNAP-AI helps eligibility workers by:
- Automating application intake and data extraction (50-70% time savings)
- Pre-screening applications for eligibility before worker review
- Validating documents and flagging missing information
- Prioritizing urgent cases (expedited eligibility, aged applications)
- Distributing workload intelligently across available staff
- Ensuring compliance with federal and state SNAP regulations

**Not a Replacement System**  
SNAP-AI is designed to work **with** county eligibility workers, not replace them. Final eligibility decisions remain with trained social services staff.

## Revenue Model

### Pricing Structure

#### Option 1: Per-Application Model
- **$X per application** processed through SNAP-AI
- Volume-based tiering (higher volume = lower per-app cost)
- Only pay for applications actually processed
- Performance guarantees tied to processing speed

#### Option 2: Monthly Subscription (SaaS)
- **County-Level Pricing**
  - Small counties (<5,000 SNAP households): $3,000-5,000/month
  - Medium counties (5,000-15,000): $8,000-12,000/month
  - Large counties (15,000+): $15,000-25,000/month
- Includes unlimited application processing
- Annual contracts with quarterly payment terms

#### Option 3: State Enterprise License
- **Statewide contract** covering all counties in a state
- North Carolina example: $1.5-3M annually for all 100 counties
- State negotiated pricing with federal match
- Volume discounts for multi-year commitments

### Additional Revenue Streams

- Custom configuration for state-specific rules
- Ongoing support and maintenance
- Analytics and reporting enhancements
- Multi-benefit expansion (Medicaid, TANF, Child Care)

## Implementation Strategy

- Requirements gathering with Cumberland County DSS
- Integration with NC FAST (North Carolina's SNAP system)
- Security and compliance review
- Staff training program development

**Month 3-4: Pilot Launch**
- Deploy to 10-15 eligibility workers
- Process 1,000-2,000 applications
- Daily monitoring and optimization
- Weekly feedback sessions with workers

**Month 5-6: Expansion & Optimization**
- Expand to all Cumberland County SNAP workers (50-75 staff)

## Risk Mitigation

### Technical Risks
- **State System Integration**: Each state has different SNAP systems
  - *Mitigation*: API-first architecture, modular connectors

- **Document Processing Accuracy**: OCR errors could lead to eligibility mistakes
  - *Mitigation*: Human review of all automated extractions, continuous ML improvement

- **Data Privacy**: Handling sensitive applicant information
  - *Mitigation*: Encryption, access controls, compliance with IRS 1075 and state standards

### Business Risks
- **County Budget Constraints**: Limited IT budgets at county level
  - *Mitigation*: State-level contracts, federal match funding, ROI-based pricing

- **Change Resistance**: Workers may resist new technology
  - *Mitigation*: User-centered design, comprehensive training, worker advisory board

- **Procurement Complexity**: County/state procurement processes can be slow
  - *Mitigation*: Cooperative purchasing agreements, GSA Schedule, state contracts

### Regulatory Risks
- **Federal SNAP Rule Changes**: USDA regulations can change
  - *Mitigation*: Flexible rules engine, rapid update capability, federal compliance monitoring

- **State Approval Required**: State agencies must approve county systems
  - *Mitigation*: Early engagement with state SNAP offices, compliance documentation

## Success Criteria

### Quantitative Metrics (Cumberland County Pilot)
- **Processing Time**: 45%+ reduction in average days from application to decision
- **Timeliness Rate**: Improve from current ~75% to 85%+ (federal standard is 95%)
- **Backlog Reduction**: Clear all applications older than 60 days within 90 days
- **Worker Productivity**: 30%+ increase in applications processed per worker per day
- **Error Rate**: Maintain or improve current eligibility accuracy (target <5% error rate)

- **Worker Satisfaction**: Positive feedback on reduced workload stress
- **Supervisor Support**: Endorsement from DSS leadership
- **Client Experience**: Improved applicant satisfaction with processing speed
- **State Recognition**: NCDHHS endorsement for statewide expansion
- **Federal Recognition**: USDA FNS best practice recognition
